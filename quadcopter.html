Perching Quadcopter

Perching like a bird.

In this project we design a quadcopter that can do something that's never been attempted: land like a bird. A robotic end effector is mounted on a Sky Specs quadcopter and is used to grab on to a bar for landing. Although the quadcopter has an onboard flight controller, a pilot will still struggle to keep the quadcopter in place because there are external forces acting on it such as decreases in air pressure when a door opens or ground effects. We designed an "outer-loop" controller that adjusts thrust, roll, pitch, and yaw and replaces the pilot control; this allows the quadcopter to hold its location precisely and to navigate to virtual waypoints. We also implemented a state machine that identifies when the quadcopter is close to the bar and initiates a landing sequence.

Tuning, tuning, tuning...

By far the most time consuming portion of the project was tuning the controller so that the quadcopter could hold its position on a waypoint. An Optitrack System locates the quadcopter via five infrared reflective dots on the quadcopter and sends the position data to the quadcopter's Beaglebone by ethernet. In the videos below you'll see two spotters holding cables attached to the quadcopter; they're for safety, in case the quadcopter malfunctions, and for the ethernet cable. We initialized our PID feedback controller values based on a pilot's input when he tried to keep the quadcopter hovering in place; the control values were processed in Matlab Simulink's System Identification Toolbox. Next, we would record test flight data and try to understand which values needed to be tuned. For example, if the quadcopter navigated to the setpoint but drifted slowly backwards, we would increase the differential-term of the pitch controller.

In the second video you can see that the quadcopter is more stable under autonomous control than in the pilot's hands.

Gripper Design

The gripper needed to be able to keep the quadcopter on the rod with up to a 6 degree tilt (no landing is perfect!). Based on the weight of the quadcopter we designed a gripper with a little over 2 Nm. of gripping torque. The blue pads on the finger tips are silicone rubber for increased friction. The gripper was 3D printed using ABS plastic and is actuated by one AX-12 servo.

Competition Day

On the day of competition we were able to accomplish all the tasks: autonomous position-hold, waypoint navigation, autonomous perching and liftoff.
