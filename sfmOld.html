Recreating 3D Scene from 2D Images

Structure from Motion (SfM) takes a series of images, such as a video, and recreates the scene in 3D (structure) and traces the path that the camera took through the environment (motion). The structure-from-motion problem has only recently been solved yet it's impact in various fields has been enormous. It has allowed researchers to map out an entire city using pictures crowd-sourced from Flickr and Hollywood directors to insert computer generated characters into their scenes without green screens.

Extracting Features and the Fundamental Matrix

To correlate the motion between several images we must first extract the features between pairs of images. We use can use the Harris Corner Detector or the more robust Speeded-Up-Robust-Features (SURF) detector. By finding matching points between images we can begin to find the relationship between the cameras, which is embedded in the Fundamental Matrix. The Fundamental Matrix operates on a point on Image 1 and creates corresponding epipolar line on Image 2, which should pass through the matching point on Image 2.
